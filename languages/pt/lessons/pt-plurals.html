Plural Formation in Portuguese

Learn how to make nouns and adjectives plural

Basic Rules

In Portuguese, most plurals are formed by adding -s to words ending in a vowel. However, there are several important patterns to learn.

Ending Rule Singular Plural
Vowel Add -s casa casas
-r, -s, -z Add -es flor flores
-al, -el, -ol Change -l to -is animal animais
-il Change -il to -eis fácil fáceis
-m Change -m to -ns jovem jovens
-ão Change to -ões limão limões

Quick Tip

Words ending in -s in the singular form don't change in the plural: o lápis → os lápis (the pencil → the pencils)

Special Cases: -ão Endings

Three Patterns for -ão

Words ending in -ão can have three different plural forms, with -ões being by far the most common:

  • -ão → -ões (approximately 80% of cases): botão → botões, limão → limões, televisão → televisões
  • -ão → -ães (less common): pão → pães, cão → cães, alemão → alemães
  • -ão → -ãos (least common): irmão → irmãos, mão → mãos, órgão → órgãos

When in doubt, -ões is usually the safest choice!

Article and Adjective Agreement

Remember that when making nouns plural, articles and adjectives must also agree! For example:

Singular: O gato preto (The black cat)

Plural: Os gatos pretos (The black cats)

Pattern Singular Plural Meaning
-ões limão limões lemon(s)
-ões botão botões button(s)
-ães pão pães bread(s)
-ães alemão alemães German(s)
-ãos irmão irmãos brother(s)
-ãos cidadão cidadãos citizen(s)

Compound Words

Important Rules

For compound words, usually only the last word changes to plural if it's a noun:

  • guarda-chuva → guarda-chuvas
  • couve-flor → couves-flores

Special Cases and Exceptions

Irregular Plurals

Some words have irregular plural forms:

  • mal → males (evil/bad)
  • cônsul → cônsules (consul)
  • gol → gols (goal in soccer/football)

Foreign Words

Loanwords usually follow simplified plural rules:

  • show → shows
  • clube (club) → clubes
  • hambúrguer → hambúrgueres

Collective Nouns and Special Words

Collective nouns are singular but refer to groups:

  • o pessoal (the staff/personnel) - always singular despite referring to many people
  • a gente (we/us) - literally means "the people" but is used as "we" and takes singular verbs
  • o povo (the people/population) - singular form for a collective group

Watch Out!

"A gente" is very common in informal Brazilian Portuguese meaning "we", but always uses singular verb forms:

Correct: A gente vai ao cinema (We're going to the movies)

Incorrect: A gente vamos ao cinema

Common Mistakes

1. Wrong -ão Plural

pãos → pães

2. Forgetting Article/Adjective Agreement

os gato preto → os gatos pretos

3. Wrong "a gente" Usage

a gente vamos → a gente vai

As pessoas estão felizes com os resultados.

→ Nós vamos encontrar _____ _________ _____ _________.

Nós vamos encontrar os capitães nos aviões.

→ _____ ________ _______ escaparam _____ _______ __________.

Os animais ferozes escaparam dos jardins zoológicos.
