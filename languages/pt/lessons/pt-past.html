Past Tense in Portuguese

Learn how to talk about past events in Portuguese

Pretérito Perfeito (Simple Past)

The pretérito perfeito is used to describe completed actions in the past. It's similar to the English simple past tense.

Quick Tip

Regular verbs in Portuguese follow predictable patterns based on their endings (-ar, -er, -ir). Learning these patterns will help you conjugate most verbs!

Subject Falar (to speak) Comer (to eat) Dormir (to sleep)
Eu falei comi dormi
Você/Ele/Ela falou comeu dormiu
Nós falamos comemos dormimos
Vocês/Eles/Elas falaram comeram dormiram

Important Note

The ending patterns are consistent within each verb group (-ar, -er, -ir). Once you learn one verb from each group, you can apply the same pattern to other regular verbs.

Irregular Verbs

Common Irregulars

Some of the most common verbs in Portuguese are irregular in the past tense. These need to be memorized separately:

  • ser/ir → fui, foi, fomos, foram
  • ter → tive, teve, tivemos, tiveram
  • fazer → fiz, fez, fizemos, fizeram

Eu fui ao mercado.

I went to the market.

Time Expressions

Common time expressions used with the past tense:

  • ontem (yesterday)
  • na semana passada (last week)
  • no mês passado (last month)
  • há dois dias (two days ago)

Usage Tip

When using time expressions with "há", remember that it indicates how long ago something happened: "há três dias" = "three days ago"

Regional Variations

The past tense usage can vary between Brazilian Portuguese and European Portuguese. Here are the key differences:

Brazilian Portuguese

  • Prefers simple past tense (pretérito perfeito) for recent actions
  • Uses "ter" as auxiliary verb
  • Example: "Eu já almocei" (I already had lunch)

European Portuguese

  • More frequent use of compound past with "ter"
  • Sometimes uses "haver" as auxiliary
  • Example: "Eu já tenho almoçado" (I already had lunch)

Cultural Note

These differences reflect broader cultural patterns in how time and completion are viewed in different Portuguese-speaking regions.

Common Mistakes

Watch out for these frequent errors when using the past tense:

1. Confusing Ser and Estar in the Past

Eu fui doente ontem → Eu estava doente ontem

Use 'estava' for temporary states in the past

2. Irregular Verb Confusion

Eu fazei → Eu fiz

Remember that 'fazer' is irregular in the past tense

3. Time Expression Errors

Eu fui lá desde dois dias → Eu fui lá há dois dias

Use 'há' to indicate time ago, not 'desde'
