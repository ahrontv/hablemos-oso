Past Tense Comparison

Understanding when to use Pretérito Indefinido vs. Imperfecto

Pretérito Indefinido

Used for:

  • Completed actions
  • Specific moments
  • Sequential events
  • Actions with clear start/end

Ayer comí una pizza.

Yesterday I ate a pizza.

Pretérito Imperfecto

Used for:

  • Habitual past actions
  • Ongoing states
  • Background information
  • Descriptions in the past

De niño, comía pizza cada viernes.

As a child, I used to eat pizza every Friday.

Side-by-Side Examples

Indefinido: Ayer llovió por dos horas.

Yesterday it rained for two hours. (Specific, completed event)

Imperfecto: Cuando era niño, llovía mucho en mi ciudad.

When I was a child, it used to rain a lot in my city. (Habitual action)

Indefinido: María se rompió el brazo jugando al fútbol.

Maria broke her arm playing soccer. (Specific incident)

Imperfecto: María jugaba al fútbol todos los días.

Maria used to play soccer every day. (Habitual action)

Using Both Tenses Together

Often, both tenses work together to tell a complete story:

Mientras estudiaba (imperfecto) en la biblioteca, Juan llegó (indefinido) con una noticia importante.

While I was studying in the library, Juan arrived with important news.

  • estudiaba - ongoing action (background)
  • llegó - specific event that interrupted

Era (imperfecto) un día soleado cuando recibí (indefinido) la carta.

It was a sunny day when I received the letter.

  • era - background description
  • recibí - specific event

Quick Reference Guide

Use Indefinido when:

  • Stating when something happened
  • Describing a specific number of times
  • Indicating start or end of action
  • Listing sequential events

Use Imperfecto when:

  • Describing how things were
  • Talking about repeated habits
  • Setting the scene
  • No specific completion time
